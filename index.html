BRAND CREATION & MARKETPLACE GROWTH CASE

Building a Korean-Minimal Brand From Zero

Built kiki&co from zero as a Korean-inspired minimalist hair styling brand by defining positioning, analyzing competitors, shaping visual direction, and supporting marketplace SEO, storefront design, content style, and paid ads for a new hair styling product line.

SITUATION

CBG’s main beauty gadget portfolio had a Japanese-inspired direction, while the company wanted to launch kiki&co as a separate Korean-inspired brand for hair styling tools. The market was crowded with brands leaning into overly feminine, cute, or princess-like aesthetics.

TASK

Create a distinct brand identity for kiki&co, define its positioning, shape the visual direction, and support the launch of a new hair styling product line that could stand apart from competitors and perform on marketplace channels.

ACTION

Defined the brand positioning and proposed a Korean-inspired minimalist direction. Conducted competitor analysis, developed moodboards, visual references, product concepts, benefit-led copy, content style, and social media tone. Briefed designers, directed Shopee/Lazada storefront design, and supported marketplace SEO and paid ads. Used keyword research to identify that customers were actively searching for standard hair straighteners, not only unusual or novelty styling tools. Recommended focusing on high-demand core hair styling products, then supported sales through marketplace optimization and performance ads.

RESULT

Launched kiki&co as a distinct brand with a clearer Korean-minimal identity and a new hair styling product line. Despite early marketplace product-upload issues, sales gained traction once the full product range went live across Shopee, Lazada, and TikTok, reaching 7-figure THB annual sales with a 500K+ THB peak month.
